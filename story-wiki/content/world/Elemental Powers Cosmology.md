# Elemental Powers Cosmology

**Used In:** [Burning Punches and Frozen Kicks](../stories/Burning Punches and Frozen Kicks.md)
**Related:** [Power Level System Design](../techniques/Power Level System Design.md)

---

## Overview

The Elemental Powers Cosmology is the underlying rule system for how fire and ice abilities work in [Burning Punches and Frozen Kicks](../stories/Burning Punches and Frozen Kicks.md). Unlike technological or trained abilities, elemental powers are **innate and identity-linked** — they manifest from within, shaped by who the fighter is rather than what they've learned.

---

## The Two Elements

### Fire — Flaming Fists
- Manifestation: Strikes, punches, close-range combat channelled through the hands
- Temperament: Aggressive, escalating, forward-momentum
- Visual: Heat distortion, fire trails on strikes
- Grows with: Intensity of will and emotional drive

### Ice — Frozen Kicks
- Manifestation: Kicks, leg-based combat, area control through freezing
- Temperament: Controlled, patient, defensive set-up
- Visual: Frost spread on impact, ice formations
- Grows with: Precision and concentration

---

## The Opposition and Complementarity

Fire and ice are **natural opposites** — they cancel each other's effects at matching intensity. But they are also complementary in combined tactics: fire opens (charges forward, creates pressure), ice closes (disables, locks down, controls space). The two elemental fighters are stronger as a unit than either is alone.

This tension — opposition vs. complementarity — mirrors the relationship between the two fighters themselves.

---

## Power Progression

Elemental power scales through life stages:
- **Childhood:** Raw manifestation — uncontrolled, instinctive, high emotional trigger
- **Teenage:** Technique development — learning to direct power, fight with precision
- **Adulthood:** Full mastery — war-level expression, ability to sustain combat against highest-rank opponents

---

## Cosmological Rules

- Elements are exclusive to the bearer — no one else shares their exact expression
- Elements cannot be taught, only developed from within
- At maximum output, both elements reshape local environment (fire scorches terrain; ice restructures it)
- Whether elements cancel or combine depends on the fighters' relationship and intent
