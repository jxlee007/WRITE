# Scene 001 — The Fourteen Days

**Location:** A planet whose name is not known in this section of the universe. Sparse population, scattered settlements, red dust. The site of a massacre. Fourteen days after.
**Characters:** Thoulien (teenage arc — his first power level has just arrived, catastrophically)
**Purpose:** Thoulien has killed everyone on this settlement — friends and enemies both, in the rage that followed her death. He knows this. He is sitting with her body, not because he can't move on, but because he is learning something that cannot be learned any faster. This is the scene where a boy becomes capable of carrying destiny — not because he grows stronger, but because he learns what his strength costs.

---

The dust was red.

It had been red before the massacre. It was still red after it. The planet didn't care. Planets rarely do.

Thoulien sat with her in the shelter they'd built in the first week here — rough-walled, low-ceilinged, the kind of place that became a home because two people decided it was a home and treated it accordingly. She was on the makeshift bed. He had arranged her the way she slept, which he knew exactly: left side, one arm folded under her chin, a slight forward curve to the spine.

He had been sitting across from her for fourteen days.

He did not eat. His species didn't require it over this timescale — a mercy or a curse, depending on how you felt about being forced to fully inhabit duration. He sat and he breathed and he looked at her and he looked out through the gap in the wall where the door used to be, at the red dust and the wreckage he'd made of this place.

On the first day, he had been angry. Not the rage that had broken through when he found her — that was gone, used up, spent on everything within three kilometres. This was a smaller, sustained anger. The kind that needs somewhere to go and finds nowhere.

On the fourth day, he understood that the anger was finished.

On the seventh day, he started to understand what he had done.

Not in the abstract — he had known in the abstract within an hour. Understanding was different. Understanding meant sitting with the specific faces: the settlement elder who had reported their location. His own ally who had looked away at the wrong moment. The child at the water pump who had seen nothing, done nothing, been simply in the wrong radial of his first and involuntary burst of Lvl 1 power. Three kilometres of radius. An involuntary burst. No target selection.

He'd had no target selection.

He understood, on day seven, that this was the thing that mattered. He could rage at the people who'd betrayed her. He could chase the grief for the rest of a very long life. But the element of what happened that belonged to him — the three-kilometre radius, the uncontrolled surge, the child at the pump — that part was his and only his and it would stay his.

On day eleven, he looked at her for a long time and thought about what she would say about the child at the pump.

He knew exactly what she would say. He knew her well enough to know exactly, and knowing exactly was the most painful part of losing someone.

She would say: *You didn't know.*

And he would say: *That's not the point.*

And she would say: *Then what is?*

And he would have to think for a long time, because she was the kind of person who asked questions that required long thinking, and the answer he arrived at after fourteen days was this: the point is control. Not power — he had power. The power had arrived fully formed and catastrophic the moment grief opened it. The point is the space between the grief and the burst. The space where a decision can be made, if you've built the interior architecture to hold one.

He had not had that architecture on the day she died.

He was building it now.

On the fourteenth day, he stood up. He looked at her one more time — the red light from the gap in the wall on her face, still and complete. He memorized it, not because he would ever forget, but because the act of memorizing was its own kind of ceremony.

He walked out.

The dust moved around his feet.

He was not finished grieving. He would not be finished for a long time, possibly ever. But grief and purpose are not opposites — he understood this now. They are different loads on the same structure, and whether the structure holds depends entirely on how it was built.

He had fourteen days of architecture.

He started walking.

---

**Consequence:** Thoulien leaves the settlement with his Lvl 1 power intact but now understood — not controlled, not mastered, but *known.* He understands for the first time that his capacity for destruction is not separate from his capacity for love. They are the same thing at different temperatures. This knowledge is what makes him trainable, eventually, into the warrior the universe will require. Without the fourteen days, he would have been powerful and uncontainable. With them, he is something rarer: powerful and choosing.
