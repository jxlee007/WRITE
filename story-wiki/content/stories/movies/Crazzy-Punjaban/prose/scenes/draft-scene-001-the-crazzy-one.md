# Scene 001 — The Crazzy One

**Location:** Two places, two times. Simran: a flat in Chandigarh, 2007. Harpreet: a courtyard in Patiala, 1994. The connection between them is new — a splitting headache that began for both women at 3:17 AM on the same morning, thirteen years apart.
**Characters:** Simran (32, 2007 — works in a bank, practical, does not believe in the supernatural), Harpreet (19, 1994 — daughter of a shop owner, has always had what her family calls "too much feeling")
**Purpose:** The connection opens. Neither woman knows what is happening. The black magic has misfired — it was aimed at someone else — and it has landed on both of them simultaneously, linking two women who have nothing in common except the face they share and a headache that won't stop.

---

**2007 — Chandigarh**

The headache woke Simran at 3:17.

She knew the exact time because she kept her phone face-up on the nightstand, and the screen lit when she reached for it. She lay still for a moment, cataloguing the pain — not migraine, not sinus, not the stress headache that arrived on loan processing days. Something else. A pressure at the back of the skull and then, strange and specific, a smell: marigolds. The kind burned at pujas.

She lived alone. She did not have marigolds.

She sat up and turned the lamp on and the smell was gone.

Her phone showed 3:17 AM and also, inexplicably, a voicemail she hadn't gotten. The missed call was from an unknown number: a Patiala area code, which meant nothing because she didn't know anyone in Patiala.

She played the voicemail.

Static. And then, barely audible under the static, a girl's voice — young, seventeen, eighteen — saying something that came through in fragments: *"...doesn't work this way... I don't want to... bhai, please, this isn't..."* and then more static, and then the sound of something breaking, and the call ended.

Simran played it again.

She sat with the phone in her hand in her lit flat in 2007 and felt, for the first time in a long time, that she did not understand something.

---

**1994 — Patiala**

The headache woke Harpreet at 3:17.

She knew the time because the kitchen clock was visible from her cot and the kitchen light was always left on because her mother was afraid of the dark. She lay on her side and the pain arrived like someone pressing a thumb into the back of her head, and with the pain came the smell — marigolds, which was wrong because it was 3 AM and puja flowers are a morning thing.

And then, vivid and complete and immediately alarming: someone else's room. Clean. White walls, a strange thin device on the table, a lamp she didn't recognize, a woman sitting up in a bed that looked nothing like any bed in her family's house, looking directly at her.

Harpreet screamed.

The woman in the vision also screamed.

This was how they found each other.

---

**2007 — Chandigarh**

Simran was halfway off the bed before she understood that she had screamed, and she hadn't screamed since she was a child, and she needed a moment to re-establish that her flat was her flat and the girl had not been in the room — the girl had been inside her head, which was worse.

She turned the lamp off.

Turned it back on.

The vision was gone. The headache was still there. And in the strange silence of her Chandigarh flat at 3:20 AM, Simran Kaur — practical, bank-employed, does not believe in the supernatural — said out loud:

"*Koi hai kya?*"

No answer.

But the smell of marigolds came back, faint and unmistakable, and something at the edge of her perception — not a sound, not a sight, but something — felt like a girl in Patiala trying to breathe quietly and not be noticed.

*She's scared,* Simran thought.

*Who,* said the part of her that processes things rationally, *is she?*

---

**Consequence:** The connection is open. Simran can't close it — it isn't something she understands well enough to manage. Harpreet can't close it either, and she is considerably more frightened, because she is nineteen and has grown up in a house where the supernatural is discussed but not in ways that include *a stranger living in my head.* The Baba who cast the original black magic has realized the misfiring. He is currently looking for the intended target. He does not yet know where the magic landed. When he finds out, his reaction will be the opposite of reassuring.
