# Scene 001 — First Spar

**Location:** A training courtyard, childhood. Late afternoon. The kind of heat that sits on everything — stone walls holding the day's warmth, dust in the air, no wind.
**Characters:** Burn (age 11), Frozen (age 11)
**Purpose:** Their first real spar. They've trained alongside each other for a year. Today, for the first time, they actually fight. And in the fighting, they discover that they are completely incompatible in method and completely matched in result. The friendship becomes real the moment one of them almost seriously hurts the other.

---

The rule was no powers.

Sifu Mehren had said it clearly: no powers, no elements, hands and feet only, controlled contact, stop when the other person calls stop. This was supposed to be a form sparring exercise. This was supposed to take five minutes.

It took four seconds for Burn to forget the rule.

He didn't even notice it happening — that was the problem. One moment he was throwing a right hook, the next the air in front of his fist was distorted by heat, and the stone wall behind Frozen showed a faint scorch mark where the strike had gone wide.

Frozen had moved.

He was three feet to the left of where he'd been, arms loose at his sides, expression unchanged. That expression — the specific blankness of it, the way it didn't flinch even when a burning fist passed close enough to feel — made Burn's temper spike, which only made the heat worse.

"You're doing it again," Frozen said.

"I didn't mean to."

"You never mean to."

"It's not like I can just—"

"I can." Frozen's voice was patient in the particular way that patience becomes its own form of aggression. "When I feel the cold come, I can wait. I can let it sit there without moving."

"Good for you."

"That's not—" Frozen stopped. Reconsidered. "I'm not trying to make you feel bad."

"You're making me feel bad."

"I'm trying to explain that control is possible."

"For you," Burn said, with the emphasis of someone who has heard this particular comparison for one full year and has decided he is very tired of it. "Control is possible *for you*. Because your power is about holding still. Mine isn't. Mine is about *going.*"

He threw another punch. No element this time — deliberate, conscious. Frozen stepped inside it and used Burn's momentum to redirect him into the dust, one smooth rotation, and Burn landed badly on his left knee and stopped himself on his palms.

The pain arrived a second later. His knee was fine. His palm was not — the reflex had fired, and the stone under his left hand was cracked and warm.

He looked at his hand. Then up at Frozen.

Frozen was looking at his palm too. His face had done the one thing it almost never did: it had changed.

"Your hand," he said.

"It's fine."

"Burn—"

"It's *fine.*" He stood up, brushing dust off his knee. His palm was red. Nothing broken, nothing serious, but the skin where the heat had discharged against the stone was tender in a way that would be worse tomorrow. "You didn't do anything. I did it myself."

Frozen crouched down and looked at the cracked stone. He touched the edge of it once, and frost crept from his fingertip along the crack, and the stone went silent the way hot metal goes silent when it cools.

He stood back up.

"Do you want to stop?" he said.

Burn looked at the frost on the stone. Looked at Frozen.

"No," he said. "I want to go again. But this time—" he rolled his shoulder — "don't let me go wide. If you see the heat start, move *into* me. Don't move away."

Frozen considered this. "That doesn't sound safe."

"If you move into me before the heat fires, you'll be inside the range and you'll be fine. If you move away, I follow, and then it's worse."

"That's a terrible training philosophy."

"It works in theory."

"You've never tested the theory."

"We're testing it now."

A long pause. The courtyard was fully quiet — everyone else had gone in for the evening meal. Just the two of them and the scorch mark and the frost-covered crack.

"Okay," Frozen said.

He raised his hands.

---

**Consequence:** They spar for another forty minutes, long past dinner. The frost and the heat leave the courtyard looking like a weather system had an argument inside it. Neither one wins. Neither one loses. Sifu Mehren finds them there at dark, both sitting on opposite sides of the courtyard, exhausted, not speaking, but not leaving either. He says nothing. He goes back inside. He writes in his notes: *These two will either save each other or destroy each other. Nothing in between.*
