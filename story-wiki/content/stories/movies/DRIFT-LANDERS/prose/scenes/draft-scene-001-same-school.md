# Scene 001 — Same School

**Location:** The underground drift circuit. A converted drainage tunnel beneath an industrial district — concrete walls, sodium lights, the smell of burnt rubber. The race has just ended.
**Characters:** Veer (undercover cop), Ghost (underground circuit champion)
**Purpose:** Veer has infiltrated the circuit to track Ghost. The race ends. Ghost wins. And in the aftermath, something happens that the operation wasn't supposed to produce: Ghost recognizes him.

---

The tunnel was still ringing.

Ghost's car — a modified white R34, the kind of white that absorbs every light source in a room — crossed the line sideways, tail out, two wheels of clear air between the passenger side and the wall, and then he corrected it with a half-turn of the wheel that looked like boredom but wasn't. You could only make a correction that clean by knowing exactly where the limit was. You had to have touched it enough times to recognize it by feel.

Veer pulled in third. His car was a black GTS — stock in appearance, modified in everything that mattered. He sat in it for a moment after the engine died, watching Ghost climb out thirty metres ahead, watching the way the circuit's inner circle came to him. The way a tide moves toward a shore.

He got out.

The post-race crowd was noise and heat and the particular social architecture of people who don't exist on paper. Veer moved through it with the practised blankness of someone who belonged, or wanted to belong, or was pretending hard enough that the difference didn't matter yet.

Ghost was leaning against his car when Veer reached him. He'd taken his gloves off. He was looking at Veer with the expression of someone solving a problem they haven't been asked.

"Third," Ghost said.

"Yeah."

"You braked early on the third turn."

"The line was tight."

"The line is always tight on the third turn. That's what the turn is *for.*" Ghost tilted his head. "You can take it flat. I've watched you on the straight — you have the pace. You're choosing to brake."

Veer looked at him. "Maybe I'm careful."

"You're not careful. Your entry angle on turn two was aggressive. You're choosing to brake on three specifically." A pause. "Because you want to stay third."

The sodium lights hummed overhead. Around them, the crowd moved and talked and didn't pay attention to two drivers having a quiet conversation by a car. But Veer felt the conversation happening at a different register than it appeared to.

"Why would I want to stay third?" he said.

Ghost looked at him for a long moment. Something in his face settled into certainty.

"I don't know yet," he said. "But I know the school."

Veer went still.

Ghost put his gloves in his jacket pocket. "Malwa Drift Academy. 2014 intake. They teach a specific hip-entry technique on tight right-handers — push through the hip before the hands, it keeps the rear loose without breaking traction. You did it on turns four, seven, and nine." He looked at Veer's car. "I did it on every turn. Because Rajan Sir taught us both."

The name landed between them like a stone dropped in water.

Veer said nothing.

Ghost smiled — not warm, not cold. Something more complicated. "You were a year ahead of me. I remember your face. I didn't remember your name until the seventh turn." He stepped away from his car, relaxed, easy, as if none of this mattered. "Come and have tea. If you want." He glanced back once. "Or don't. But either way, I know what you are."

He walked away into the crowd.

Veer stood by the black GTS for a moment, alone.

*His handler was going to have questions. The operation had a clean protocol: observe, map the network, identify the supply chain, do not get identified. He'd been here six weeks and had gotten through all of it without a single flag.*

*Ghost had clocked him in forty-five minutes.*

*Not because of the badge. Not because of the car. Because of the way he held his hips on a right-hander.*

He followed Ghost into the crowd.

---

**Consequence:** Veer blows his cover protocol by following Ghost. He tells himself it's to control the situation — if Ghost knows, he needs to know what Ghost intends to do with that information. The truth is more uncomfortable: Ghost is the first person in six weeks who has seen him clearly, and Veer has been undercover long enough that being seen — even by the man he's supposed to arrest — feels like relief.
