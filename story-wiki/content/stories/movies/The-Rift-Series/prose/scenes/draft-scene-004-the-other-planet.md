# Scene 004 — The Other Planet

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** The space between worlds. Then: the boundary of her space.
**Follows:** Scene 003 — he has crossed through the rift for the first time.
**Purpose:** He sees her planet. He sees her. She does not know he is there. He feels the particular wrongness of being somewhere he did not announce himself — not guilt exactly, but its adjacent feeling. He returns.

---

The between-space is brief.

He passes through it the way you pass through the moment between sleeping and waking — aware that something is changing, not in control of the rate. The corridor has no floor, no wall, no ceiling. It has only direction. The direction is forward, and forward leads somewhere, and he follows it.

Then he arrives.

---

He is in space again. But not his space.

He knows this immediately — the same way you know, in an unfamiliar room, that it is not yours. The dark here has a different quality. Different depth. A different atmosphere at the edges of his perception: warmer, more layered, more *inhabited* than his own. His space is a space that one person built and lived in alone. This space has been lived in differently. More openly. More facing-outward.

He turns.

Her planet is ahead.

From here it is not large — the size of something he could hold, if his hand were vast enough. But the detail of it, even from this distance, is immediately different from his own. His planet, seen from orbit, is precise: the towers in their configuration, the organised plain, the western ocean. This planet is not like that.

He cannot see all of it yet. But what he can see: it is *full*. Not in the way that suggests chaos — in the way that suggests a person who has brought many things in from many places and found room for all of them. There are colours here that don't resolve into a single palette from orbit — multiple light sources, it seems, or surfaces that reflect differently at different angles. Things growing in places that would, on his planet, be unoccupied. Connections — visible, even from here, as luminous threads running from her planet outward in several directions. Rifts, he understands slowly. She has rifts in multiple directions. She has been doing this for a long time.

And then he sees her.

She is not on the planet. She is above it — orbiting at mid-altitude, the way he sometimes stands on his walkways. Moving, but not urgently. Attending to something in her atmosphere. She is entirely absorbed in what she is doing. She does not look in his direction.

She does not know he is here.

He goes completely still.

He has been in places where he did not belong before — wrong rooms, wrong moments — and the feeling he associates with that is a specific kind of alertness. The instinct to be small, to take up less space, to not disturb whatever is happening in the room that would be happening without him. That feeling is here, but stronger. Because this is not a room. This is her world. Everything in it was built by her or brought in by her or shaped by her over what appears to be a long time. He is in it without her knowledge.

He watches her for a moment.

One moment. The length of time it takes to understand that you should not be watching.

Then he turns and goes back through the rift.

---

He is on his plain.

The rift behind him is still open — a seam in his sky, the warmth at its edges unchanged. He stood in her world for less than a minute. He was not seen. Nothing was disturbed, as far as he knows.

He feels disturbed.

Not frightened. Not ashamed, exactly. Something more specific and harder to categorise: the particular unease of having seen something real about a person — the shape of their world, the quality of their life, the way they move when they believe no one is watching — before they chose to show you any of it. Before there was any agreement that you could look.

He had not meant to do anything wrong. He hadn't known the rules existed.

He knows them now.

He looks at the rift — still open, still warm, still there — and he stands on his plain with the unease sitting in him and thinks:

*Something came and opened this in my world. And I went through. And she didn't know.*

He does not close the rift.

He goes back to his towers.

---

**Consequence:** He saw her. She doesn't know. He carries this — the image of her planet, the threads of her connections going outward in all directions, her moving through her own atmosphere with the ease of someone at home. He saw more than he was meant to see for a first encounter. He doesn't know how to account for it. The rift in his sky stays open. He does not know whether to wait near it, or avoid it, or go back through and announce himself. He goes to the towers. He refines the same problem he has always refined. He is not concentrating. For the first time in a long time, the problem in front of him is less interesting than the problem he just walked away from.
