# Scene 205 — The Ecology of Rifts

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** Between his world and hers. Both planets, both spaces. Time is compressed here — this scene covers a longer period than one visit.
**Follows:** Scene 204 — he has made his first intentional reach. Time continues to pass.
**Purpose:** He witnesses, across multiple visits and from his own planet's space, the living nature of connection: a rift fades, a rift brightens, a new rift forms elsewhere. He understands that connection is not a state — it is a process, alive, requiring tending or releasing. And through this understanding, he and she grow closer.

---

He watches a rift die.

He is on his planet when he notices it — one of her threads, visible from his eastern sky when he looks in her direction from high altitude, has been dimming over the weeks in the way a lamp dims when its source is slowly running low. He noticed it three visits ago. He noticed it again two visits ago. Now, from orbit above his own planet, he can see that the thread is barely visible — a faint trace where a connection used to run, still technically present, but only just.

He does not know who or what is on the other side of that thread. It is not his rift to mourn.

But he watches it.

Over the next few days, from his own space, he watches the thread continue to dim. At a certain point — he cannot see exactly when, because it happens gradually and then suddenly, the way these things do — the thread is gone. The direction it ran is dark. The space is empty where something used to be.

Her world, when he visits after, looks the same. She has not rearranged anything to account for the absence. The other rifts are where they were. She moves through her world with the same ease. She visits the wide rift, tends it with care, moves on. She passes through the empty direction once — just once, briefly, a slow orbit of her own planet that takes her through that space — and then continues.

He watches her pass through it.

She does not perform grief. She does not perform nothing. She simply moves through the space that used to have something in it, and then moves on, and it is exactly as complicated and exactly as simple as that.

---

Three weeks after the thread disappears, a new one begins.

He almost misses it. He is watching the space around her planet from his own orbit — a habit now, a form of attention he has developed — and in a direction he has not seen anything before, there is the beginning of a warmth. Not a rift. The pre-rift state he now knows by feel: the mark in the fabric, the touching of two resonances across distance. Something is reaching toward her world from a new direction. Something she has reached toward, or that has found her, or both.

He watches it, over days, become something more than a mark.

The warmth brightens. The mark in the fabric begins to have an edge. The space between her world and whatever is in the new direction starts to prepare itself — the way space prepares, the thickening and orientating that comes before a seam forms. He has seen this from the inside, from his own reaching. He recognises it from the outside now.

By the end of the following week, there is a new thread running from her planet in the new direction. Thin, still. New. But real.

He sits in his own orbit and looks at the full field of her connections — the ones he knows by now, by quality and direction and approximate age, and this new one, unfamiliar, belonging to something he doesn't know yet.

Her world is always becoming.

His world is becoming too, though more slowly — the mark he made to the north of his planet has, over the same weeks, been developing its own pre-rift quality. He tends it as she showed him: light reaches, patient holds, clean releases. The warmth in the northern direction has continued to orient toward him, continued to deepen. He does not know if it will become a rift. He does not know the timeline of these things, or if there is one. He tends it and releases and lets it move at whatever pace the space between them requires.

---

He visits her world more often now.

Not always formally — not always the full crossing, the arrival, the time spent together. Sometimes he goes to the boundary of her space and listens from there, without going in. Sometimes he arrives and she is attending to one of her rifts and he finds a place on her open ground and sits, and does his own listening from that position, reading the space around her planet from the inside. She is aware of him in those moments. She does not require him to be doing anything specific. He does not require her to be doing anything specific.

This is new — this mode of being. Being in someone's space without agenda. He has lived alone on his planet for a very long time and all of that time he has been doing something: refining, arranging, building, attending. The idea of being somewhere and simply being there was not available to him before. It is available now.

He sits on her open ground while she tends to a rift he does not know, and he does his own listen, and the quality of the time between them is something he does not have a word for because it is not a thing his world had previously required a word for.

He notes, from the inside of this time: this is what the strong rifts look like from within. The wide rift, the heavy one on her planet, the one he has seen her tend with such consistent care — it looks like this from inside, probably. Not dramatic. Not urgent. Just: two worlds, both present, both doing their own things, in the same space at the same time, and the being-in-the-same-space being the thing itself.

He looks at the rift between their worlds from inside her planet and it is brighter from here than it is from his side.

He notes this too.

---

**Consequence:** He has seen the full ecology — a connection ending, a connection beginning, connections maintained and tended across many visits. He understands now that connection is not an event. It is a condition you live in, that requires returning to, that changes whether you tend it or not. His own skills have grown across this time — the northern pre-rift is developing, his listen is more precise, his light reach is now almost effortless. But the most significant development is this: he has learned to be in someone's space without needing the time to be anything particular. That is not a skill she taught him. That is something that happened while she was teaching him other things. It is its own kind of progress.
