# Scene 301 — The Misunderstanding

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** Her planet. The rift between their worlds.
**Follows:** Scene 206 — the peak. Everything was as good as it has been. Something changes.
**Purpose:** Something happens between them. The specific nature of the misunderstanding is visual — shown through the behaviour of their worlds, not through words. He misreads something. He does not stay to understand it. He leaves before she can show him what he missed.

---

He arrives at her planet to find the wide rift — the substantial one, the one that is not theirs — in a state he has not seen before.

It is bright. Brighter than any of her rifts usually appear from inside her atmosphere. Not unstable — not fraying, not fluctuating. Simply very present. Very *active*. Whatever is on the other side of that rift is, right now, sending something toward her world with a particular intensity. The resonance is strong enough that he can feel it from across her open ground, without approaching the rift, without intending to read it. It simply arrives in his awareness: something significant is happening there.

She is at the edge of it.

Her back is to him. She does not know he has arrived — or she knows, but she is in the middle of something and the middle is where her attention is. She is at the edge of the wide rift with both hands near its surface, and the warmth coming off it at this distance and intensity is something he has not felt from it before.

He stands on her open ground and watches.

He watches her with the wide rift. He watches the quality of her attention — complete, absorbed, the same quality she has when something matters to her. He has seen her at this rift many times. He has never seen it look like this.

He does not approach.

Something in the quality of the moment — the brightness of the rift, her complete absorption, the warmth that is not directed at him and is not his — produces the feeling he has been carrying since Scene 202, the one he never found a name for. It is the same feeling, but louder. Not painful. Not yet. But larger. Taking up more space in him than it has before.

He waits.

She does not turn around.

He waits longer, on the open ground, not moving, not approaching, not announcing himself more clearly than his presence already does. His presence always does something in her world — the atmosphere acknowledges him now, the way a room acknowledges the person who is regularly in it. She should feel that. She knows when he is here.

She does not turn around.

This is the moment. Not the wide rift, not the intensity of the resonance coming through it. This moment: him waiting, her absorbed, and the waiting going on long enough that something in him makes a decision without consulting him fully.

He turns.

He goes back through their rift. His rift. The wide, warm, stable thing they built together, which is as bright and large as it has ever been.

He goes through it.

He is on his own plain.

He does not look back at the rift in his eastern sky. He walks toward the towers. The quality of his walking is something he has not shown before — not the careful deliberate movement of a person taking time. Fast. The walking of a person who wants to be somewhere other than where they were.

---

**Consequence:** He left. He does not know what he actually saw — or rather, he knows what he saw but not what it meant, and he did not stay to find out. She was tending a rift with intensity. He interpreted the intensity as something. The interpretation may be correct or it may not be. The story does not tell us. What matters is that he chose not to stay to learn the difference. He chose not to wait one more minute, or go to her, or extend his presence more clearly so she would turn. He chose, for the first time since this began, to leave before understanding was possible. What happens next is entirely the consequence of that choice.
