# Scene 202 — Fascinating / Unsettling

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** Her planet. His own planet. The movement between them.
**Follows:** Scene 201 — he has seen the full scope of her connections. He is processing.
**Purpose:** He watches her tend to her rifts over time. The observation produces two feelings he carries simultaneously: fascination at the living ecology of connection, and something harder to name — the sensation of encountering a thing that is completely outside his experience, which is both wondrous and disorienting.

---

He comes back more than once.

This is new for him — the returning. His world is a world of returning to the same problems, the same towers, the same refined questions. But the returning he knows is circular: back to the same thing, from the same angle, to do the same work. This returning is different. Each time he comes back to her world, something in it has shifted. A rift has changed. Something is different in the quality of one of the threads.

He watches her tend to them.

---

She does not tend to all of them equally. This is the first thing he notices. Some rifts she approaches often — the stronger ones, the ones that require maintenance not because they are fragile but because they are active, currently being used, carrying something back and forth between her world and another. She spends time at these. Not working, exactly — it is not effortful. More the way you spend time in a place that you genuinely want to be in, because the company of it is worthwhile.

Some rifts she barely visits. The thin ones. He watches her pass near one of the thinner threads without stopping, without attending to it. The thread does not fade. It maintains itself on whatever minimum resonance it runs on. She does not neglect it — she simply does not need to tend it. It is what it is: a faint connection with something distant that has found its sustainable level and holds there.

He finds himself wondering about the things on the other side.

This is new territory for him — the wondering about other worlds, other people, what they have built and how they move through their own space. Before he found the rift in Scene 001, he had not thought in this direction at all. Now he stands at the edge of one of her active rifts and feels, faintly, the presence of something on the other side — a warmth that is not hers, a specific gravity that is not his — and he wonders what that world looks like. What kind of person builds it.

He pulls back from the edge.

That is not his rift. Not his to enter or to follow or to investigate.

But the wondering stays.

---

She is at the wide rift — the substantial one, the one that is currently active — when he sees something he has not seen before.

She is reaching toward it. Not toward the interior of it, not stepping through. Reaching at the edge — the gesture he now knows: the slight extension, the leaning of attention. And the rift is responding, its edge brightening slightly, the warmth at the seam intensifying. She is doing something — he cannot tell what from where he stands, cannot follow the specific exchange — but something is being communicated between her world and the world on the other side. Something is being sent, or received, or maintained.

He watches this for a long time.

The feeling it produces in him is not simple. It is two things at once, and they do not cancel each other out.

The first thing: wonder. Pure and structural. The way he feels when a problem resolves into a solution he had not anticipated — the specific satisfaction of something real being revealed. She is tending to a connection the way a person tends to something alive, with care and attention and time, and the connection is responding. It is responsive. It is a living thing. The fact of this is genuinely extraordinary. He did not know, before any of this began, that such things existed. The world is larger than his planet by a distance he still does not have units for.

The second thing: something he has no exact name for.

He stands at the edge of her world and watches her tend to a connection that is not with him, with someone whose world he cannot see and whose resonance he can only partially feel from outside the rift, and there is something in this that sits awkwardly in his chest. Not painfully. Not jealously, which is a word he knows from his internal library but which does not quite fit the feeling. Something closer to: *I did not know that this kind of thing was possible for a person to have, and she has had it for what appears to be a long time, and I am new to all of this, and the gap between her experience and mine is very large.*

He is aware, for the first time, that he has arrived late to something.

He does not think of this as a problem — it is simply a fact. But it is a fact that has a texture to it. He has always been very good at the things he does. He has refined his towers for a long time. He is not accustomed to being a beginner.

He is a beginner here.

She finishes what she is doing at the wide rift and turns. She sees him watching. She reads something in his stillness — she is very good at reading the quality of stillness, he has noticed this — and she comes toward him.

She does not ask what he is feeling. She simply arrives beside him and stands there, in the companionable parallel stance she has adopted whenever he needs time, and waits.

He looks at her connections. All of them. The full panorama.

He looks at her.

She looks back at him with the expression of a person who has been through this particular realisation before — not his realisation specifically, but the shape of it. The moment when something opens up and the opening is both wonderful and too large.

She is not bothered by the largeness.

He is trying not to be.

---

**Consequence:** He carries both feelings back to his world — the wonder and the unnamed discomfort — and sits with them in the towers. His planet's lean toward her space has increased. The rift in his eastern sky is brighter than it was when Episode 2 began. The emotions are not resolved. They are simply present: two real things that coexist, the way two weights on the same structure coexist if the structure is built to carry both. He is building toward that. He is not there yet.
