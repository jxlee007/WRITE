# Scene 203 — The Teaching

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** Her planet. The open ground, away from the rifts — a space she has chosen specifically because it is not near any of her connections, which means there is no interference, nothing pulling at the atmosphere except what they deliberately introduce.
**Follows:** Scene 202 — the wonder and the unnamed feeling have settled into readiness. He is ready to learn properly.
**Purpose:** She teaches him the full shape of the skill — not just the reaching gesture she showed him in Scene 006, but the range of it. How to feel proximity. How to distinguish a resonance from a registration. How to extend and hold without forcing. How to release without closing. He learns by watching her do it, and then doing it himself, and then failing, and then doing it again.

---

She chooses the open ground.

He notices this — that she moves them away from her rifts, to the part of her world that is most empty of connection. He understands why when he arrives there and the air is different: quieter, less weighted, the warmth of her world present without the layered warmth of the active threads running through it. Here, in the relative silence of the open ground, the things they produce between them will be readable. Nothing to confuse the signal.

She faces him.

She begins.

---

The first thing she shows him is not the reach. It is the listen.

She stands very still and does something with her attention — he can see it without being able to describe it precisely. The quality of her concentration changes. She is not looking at him, not looking at anything visible. She is listening to the space around her planet. Attending to it. The way you attend to a sound that is at the edge of audibility: turning toward it not with your eyes but with your entire being, removing everything else from the foreground.

He watches. After a moment he understands what she is doing: she is feeling the space around her world. The full field of it. Where the warmths are, where the coolings are, which directions have something in them and which are simply empty.

She holds it for a long time, then releases.

She looks at him: *your turn.*

He tries it. He stands on the open ground of her world and turns his attention outward — past the surface, past her atmosphere, into the space around her planet. He is new at this and the newness shows immediately: instead of the quiet panoramic sweep she demonstrated, his attention goes in one direction. The direction of his own planet, his own world, pulling at him from wherever it sits in the space relative to hers. He cannot seem to extend the attention further. It keeps returning to what it knows.

She watches him struggle with this for a while. She does not intervene. She gives him the full time of the struggle, which is its own kind of teaching — letting him find the edge of the problem so he knows exactly where the edge is.

He stops. He looks at her.

She does not look frustrated. She looks like a person who expected this specific problem and is prepared to address it.

She moves to stand beside him — not facing him, but beside him, facing the same direction. She extends her attention in the way she demonstrated, the panoramic sweep, and he can feel it happening next to him: a slight shift in the atmosphere of the open ground, a change in what the space around them is doing, as she moves her awareness through it. He tries to do what she is doing, from the same position, facing the same direction.

It is slightly easier. The direction of his own planet is still the loudest thing — but beside her, with her attention already out there and patterning the space, he can follow the shape of her sweep. He extends his own attention alongside hers, imprecisely, incompletely, but in roughly the same motion.

He finds the edge of one of her other rift-threads.

Just the edge — a faint warmth from an unexpected direction, registering at the periphery of his extended attention. He has never felt this before from inside her world. From outside he had found the warmth of her world as a whole; from inside, standing on the open ground, the individual signals from the different directions her connections run are briefly, partially, readable to him.

He releases. He looks at her.

She nods. The same nod as Scene 006 — *ordinary, expected, continue.*

---

The reaching comes next.

She demonstrates the full version now, not the simplified version from Scene 006. He watches carefully. The gesture is the same at its core — the leaning of attention outward, the extension of his world's presence toward a direction — but the full version has gradations he had not seen before.

There is a *light* extension: the smallest possible reach, the equivalent of a presence rather than a contact. Something that says *I am here* without saying *I am trying to connect.* This is what you do when you feel a warmth in a direction and want to understand it without startling it. The atmospheric equivalent of standing very still near something you don't want to scare.

There is a *medium* extension: a genuine reaching, a deliberate sending of resonance toward another world. This is what forms new rifts — or begins to. It is what his unconscious reaching in Scene 001 accidentally did, without his knowledge. Deliberately, it is more controlled. More directed. He can feel, watching her demonstrate it, that the direction matters enormously — the extension has to be aimed at a specific warmth, not broadcast generally. Broadcasting generally does nothing. Directed resonance does everything.

There is a *hold*: once a resonance is sent and something responds, there is the matter of sustaining it without either pushing too hard (which overwhelms the other world's response and makes it pull back) or going too quiet (which lets the resonance fade before it has time to become anything). The hold is the most delicate part. It requires something he has in abundance — patience — and something he is less practiced at — the willingness to be in an uncertain state without resolving it prematurely.

And there is the *release*: letting the reaching go, when the time is right, without closing the connection that was built. This is the distinction between ending a reach and closing a rift. He can stop extending without ending what the extending created. The rift — if one formed — will continue on its own momentum, as long as both worlds continue to maintain some resonance with each other. The release is not a goodbye. It is just the end of the active effort.

He practices each stage.

It takes a long time. He is not naturally expressive outward — his world has been built inward, his attention has moved inward for a long time, and asking it to move outward in controlled gradations is asking a trained instrument to play notes it has never been asked for before. He fails at the hold twice. He overshoots the light extension into a medium one, which startles something at the edge of one of her threads and she steps in briefly to settle it. He is embarrassed by this. She is not. She shows him the light extension again, slower.

He gets it right on the fourth try.

He holds the light extension for a full minute — his attention in the outward direction, present but not pressing, saying *I am here* to the space without demanding any response. The space does not respond. That is not the point. The point is the quality of the reach, not the result. He holds it and releases it cleanly, and the space around them is unchanged, and he has learned the difference between trying and forcing.

She sees this.

She does not celebrate. She sits down on the open ground — which he has not seen her do before, the sitting, the choosing to be level with the surface — and she looks at the sky of her world with the expression of a person who is at rest.

He sits down beside her.

The sky above them is her sky — the atmosphere of her world, the threads of her connections visible from below as a kind of luminous filigree running in all their directions. He looks at them from here, from the ground, from the position of someone who has been given time and space to learn, by a person who clearly knows how to give those things.

He has been here many times now. Her world no longer feels foreign. It feels like a place he has come to know, which is different from a place that is his, but not so different that it doesn't have its own kind of belonging.

---

**Consequence:** He has the full skill now — not mastery, which will take time, but understanding. He knows the listen, the light reach, the medium reach, the hold, and the release. He knows the difference between extending and forcing, between releasing and closing. He has the vocabulary his hands needed to do something his world has always been capable of but never been asked for. He goes back to his own planet with this vocabulary. In Scene 204, he uses it for the first time alone.
