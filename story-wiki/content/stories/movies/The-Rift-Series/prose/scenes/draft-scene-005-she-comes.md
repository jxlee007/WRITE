# Scene 005 — She Comes

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** The outer boundary of his atmosphere. The stratosphere of his planet.
**Follows:** Scene 004 — he returned from her world, went back to his towers, has been unable to concentrate. More time has passed.
**Purpose:** He notices something orbiting near his world. He goes carefully to the boundary to look. It is her. She came to him, the way he came to her — except she is not hiding. She is simply present, looking. He goes to her immediately.

---

He has been at the towers for too long.

He knows this because the problem he has been refining has not moved. The same calibration, adjusted in the same direction, returning to the same result. His hands are doing the work and his attention is somewhere else — somewhere in the direction of the rift still hanging in his eastern sky, or further, in the direction of what he saw through it. He has not gone back to look at the rift. He has not decided what it would mean to go back.

He is on the highest walkway, the one that gives the fullest view, when he notices it.

Not immediately. It arrives at the edge of his perception first, the way the feeling always arrives — atmospheric, inexact, more like a change in temperature than a visible thing. He has learned, over the past days, to pay attention when the feeling arrives. He stops refining. He looks up.

The sky is his sky — familiar, the usual depth.

But at the outermost edge of his atmosphere, where his world shades into the open space beyond, something is moving. Something that is not his weather, not a storm, not any atmospheric event he has produced. Something with its own trajectory. Orbiting.

He rises.

---

He does not go fast. He rises through his atmosphere steadily, watching the thing at the edge as he approaches it, trying to understand what he is seeing before he reaches it. Whatever it is, it is not a rift — it has no seam, no warmth-without-source. It is a presence. A moving presence that has a direction and an intention.

He reaches the outermost layer of his atmosphere, where the air of his world becomes thin and the dark of the space beyond begins.

He stops.

She is there.

She is at the boundary — on the outside of it, in his space, moving slowly in an arc around the upper edge of his world. Not aggressively. Not invasively. The way you walk slowly around something you are trying to understand from the outside — patient, curious, keeping a respectful distance. She is looking at his planet. She has not yet looked in his direction.

He recognises her.

Not her face — he saw her from too great a distance for that. He recognises the way she moves. The ease of it. The same ease he noticed when she was orbiting her own planet, absorbed in her own atmosphere, unaware of him. She moves through space the way she moves through her own world: as if she belongs wherever she currently is.

She is orbiting his world.

Looking at it.

He understands, with a shock that is not dramatic but is complete, that she saw him. In her world. She knew he was there. She said nothing — she was not there to say anything — but she knew, and she followed the rift back, and now she is here, looking at what he built, the way he looked at what she built, except that she is not hiding.

She is simply here. In the open. Visible.

He crosses the boundary of his atmosphere into his own space.

She turns.

She sees him immediately — she was looking for him. There is a moment of stillness between them. Two people in the space between their worlds, having seen each other's planets, neither of them in possession of enough language yet for what this situation is.

Then he moves toward her.

Not cautiously. He moves the way a person moves when they have decided that caution has had its turn and something else is needed now. He moves toward her and she holds still, which is her way of meeting him — not retreating, not advancing, holding the space between them steady while he closes it.

He stops when he is close enough that the warmth he has been following for days — the directional warmth that turned his planet's lean, that pulsed once at the edge of the rift before he stepped through — is present and specific and coming from her. From her world, which is her.

They are in his space. They are at the boundary of his world.

She looks at him with the particular expression of a person who has known something was coming and waited for it to arrive in its own time.

He looks at her with the particular expression of a person who has never encountered something like this before and is doing his best to take it in at the correct rate.

Neither of them moves.

Then she turns — a slight movement, a small invitation — and gestures. Not at his world. Away from it. Toward her space. Toward the direction from which she came.

*Follow me.*

---

**Consequence:** He follows. For the first time, the crossing is not an accident, not a trespass, not a careful hesitation on a plain. It is a response to an invitation. He and she cross together — through the corridor between worlds — toward her planet. What he finds there is Scene 006.
