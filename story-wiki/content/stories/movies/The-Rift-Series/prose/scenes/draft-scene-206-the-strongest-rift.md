# Scene 206 — The Strongest Rift

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** The rift between their worlds. Both spaces. Both planets, as seen from the corridor between them.
**Follows:** Scene 205 — time has passed, connections have grown, he has changed.
**Purpose:** Episode 2 ends. The rift between them is now the most significant thing in either of their spaces — the widest, the warmest, the most continuously tended. Both worlds have changed because of it. This scene is the peak. The high point that makes Episode 3's fall possible and real.

---

The rift between their worlds is wide now.

He notices this from orbit above his own planet, on a day when the quality of the space in the eastern direction is different from its usual — not the warmth, which has been constant for a long time now, but the *size* of it. The seam that first appeared as a trace has become something you could walk through without hesitation. The warmth at its edges is not subtle. It is present. It is the first thing he registers when he rises above his atmosphere, before he looks at anything else.

He goes to it.

---

He crosses more easily than he ever has. The corridor between their worlds is familiar now — he knows the particular quality of the between-space, the brief directionless travel, the exact moment when her world's warmth replaces the neutral space of the corridor. He arrives in her space without adjustment, without the recalibration of early visits. He is at home here, in the sense that being somewhere consistently over a long time makes it yours in a different way from building it.

Her rift — their rift, seen from her side — is equally wide here.

She is near it when he arrives. She sees him come through and she is very still for a moment — the quality of her stillness is different from any he has seen from her before. Not the patient stillness of waiting. The still of someone who is looking at something and finding it completely as it should be.

He comes and stands beside her and they look at the rift together from her side.

From here, through it, he can see the edge of his own planet. His sky. A narrow slice of the dark he built, visible through the opening as if through a window. His eastern rim, the place he has stood for weeks doing his listens, is there — the specific texture of his world's edge recognisable even from this angle.

He has never seen his own planet through a rift before.

He looks at it for a long time. The slice of his world, seen from hers, through the connection between them. It looks — he tries to understand what it looks like, from this angle. It looks smaller than it feels from inside. The towers, barely visible at this distance, are exactly as he built them: precise, functional, beautiful in their precision. The weather is doing what it does. The ocean at the far edge, not visible from here but implied by the quality of the light.

He looks at his world through her eyes, from her side, and it is the closest he has come to seeing it the way she sees it.

She reaches past him, gently, and does something at the edge of the rift — a small adjustment, the kind of tending she does instinctively, the way you adjust a door that's drifted slightly in its frame. The rift settles under her attention. Its edges, which have been slowly widening over the weeks, find their current position and hold.

He watches her do this.

He understands that she has been tending this rift from her side all along — not just participating in it, but actively maintaining it. The width of it, the stability of it, the quality of its warmth is partly hers. She has been reaching back, every time he reached. He has known this in theory since Scene 006. He feels it now, watching her hands at the edge of the rift that connects their worlds, as a specific and real and enormously significant fact.

He puts his own hand to the edge.

She moves her hand beside his.

Both of them, at the edge of the same rift, on her side. His world visible through it. Her world around them.

The rift is, from the inside, like a threshold — the specific quality of a place that is the meeting point of two separate things. Not a wall. Not an open field. A threshold. It has gravity on both sides, warmth on both sides, the presence of two worlds in it simultaneously. Standing at it from her side with his hand at the edge, he can feel both: the pull of his own world, familiar and specific, and the warmth of hers, which is different and present and equally real.

He has been between these two pulls for a long time now. He is comfortable between them. The between is a place he knows.

He looks at her.

She is looking at the rift — at the slice of his world through it, at the threshold, at whatever she sees from where she is. Then she looks at him and the quality of her looking is the same as it has been from the beginning: complete attention, with patience inside it, with the ease of a person who has been here — here, in this particular kind of looking — before.

He has not been here before.

He is here now.

His planet, below and behind and through the rift in front of him, is different from when this began. Not visually — the towers are where they were, the plain is where it was. But the eastern sky has a rift in it that is wide and bright and the most significant object in his space. And his lean, which began as a barely perceptible tilt of atmosphere weeks ago, is now deliberate — he faces east when he stands on the rim, he reads east first when he listens, he knows east by the warmth of it before he orients in any other direction.

His world is more connected than it has ever been in its entire existence.

The northern pre-rift has, over the past weeks, become a thread. Thin, new, and his — the first rift he made intentionally with his own reaching, now existing in his space as a real thing. He has one other warmth he has been listening to, a different direction, that is beginning to orient. His world is facing outward, slowly, in the way of something that has practiced turning and found that turning is possible.

All of this began with a rift he found in the dark, by accident, and did not know the name for.

He looks at the rift between their worlds.

He looks at her.

He does not look away.

---

**Consequence:** Episode 2 ends here. Both worlds are at their peak connection — with each other, and with others beyond each other. The rift between them is wide, warm, stable, and tended from both sides. He has learned the skill, found his first connections, understood the ecology. She has given him all of this: the vocabulary, the time, the space to be a beginner and become something more. What he does not know — what neither of them knows — is that Episode 3 has already begun in the space between what he understood in this scene and what he is capable of hearing when something goes wrong. The bridge is at its widest. That is when burning it becomes possible.
