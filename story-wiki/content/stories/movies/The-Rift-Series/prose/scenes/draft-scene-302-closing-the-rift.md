# Scene 302 — Closing the Rift

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** His planet. The rift in his eastern sky.
**Follows:** Scene 301 — he returned to his planet and did not look back. Time has passed. He has been in his towers.
**Purpose:** He closes the rift. Deliberately, using the skill she taught him, inverted — a withdrawal instead of a reach, a taking-back instead of an extension. He does not do it in anger. He does it in the specific cold of a decision that has been made and will not be revisited. This is the first time he uses the skill to destroy.

---

He has been in the towers for a long time.

Not working. Or: working in the way of a person who is doing the motions of a task without the task being the point. His hands move. The calibration does not advance. He is not in the towers for the towers.

He is not looking at the eastern sky.

He has not looked at it since he came through.

This lasts for some time. How long, the planet does not measure in useful units — it measures in storms, and the storms are irregular now, coming faster than their usual cycle, hitting harder in the places they always hit. His weather is doing something he has not explicitly asked it to do. He watches this, from the walkway, and understands it the way he understands most things about his planet: it knows what is happening before he makes a decision.

He makes the decision.

---

He goes to the eastern rim.

The rift is there. Wide, warm, the most significant object in his sky, exactly as he left it. From here, through it, there is the between-space, the corridor, and somewhere beyond the corridor her world with its warmth and its connections and the wide rift that is not his, bright at the moment he turned away.

He faces it.

He does the listen — not to find anything new, not the panoramic sweep for proximity. He uses the listen now as a starting position, a stillness to stand in before doing what he came to do. He has only ever used it as a beginning. He stands in it for a moment.

Then he reaches — but not outward.

He reaches inward. Toward the rift from the inside, the way you reach toward a door from the hinge side. What she showed him was how to extend his world's presence toward another world, how to build the resonance that creates and maintains a rift. What he is doing now is the inverse: he is withdrawing his world's presence from the rift. Pulling back the resonance. Not from the corridor, not from the between-space — from his own end. His world's contribution to the seam, the warmth he has been sending through it for weeks, the atmospheric lean of his planet toward the east that has made the rift between their worlds as wide and stable as it is.

He takes it back.

It is not fast. The rift is substantial — it was built over time, tended from both sides, and the taking-back from one side only is like removing one wall of a structure that had two. It does not collapse immediately. It narrows. The warmth at the edges pulls slightly toward him, toward his world, away from the seam. The width decreases.

He continues.

He holds the withdrawal the way he held the reach — steadily, at a consistent level, not forcing, not rushing. The rift narrows further. The quality of the seam changes: where it was warm-edged and open, it begins to have the quality of something under pressure, the two sides of the opening being drawn back toward each other without closing, holding the tension of a thing that is being reduced but has not yet decided to finish reducing.

He keeps going.

The rift is half its former width.

A quarter.

He feels, from the other side — faintly, through the narrowing seam — something. An orientation. An awareness. Something on the other side of the rift that has noticed what he is doing and is sending something toward it — not a closing, not a reciprocal withdrawal. An opening. A reaching. Something extending toward the narrowing seam from her side, as if trying to hold it.

He does not stop.

He continues the withdrawal.

The rift narrows to a thread. The warmth at its edge is barely there. The thread holds for a moment — the faint reaching from her side against the withdrawal from his, a tension between two intentions in the same seam — and then his intention is larger than the thread can hold and the thread closes.

The eastern sky is dark in the place where the rift was.

He stands at the rim and looks at the dark.

The dark is exactly the dark of his space — the familiar dark he built, the dark of a world that exists in its own private universe. But it is in the east, and it is where something was, and it is different from the dark in all the other directions because it is a dark he made.

He made it.

He turns and goes back to the towers.

---

**Consequence:** The rift is closed from his side. It cannot be reopened from his side without him choosing to reopen it. Whether it can be approached from her side — whether she can reach toward where it was, whether a trace of it exists in the space, whether the corridor persists as a possibility or disappears entirely — he does not know. He did not think about this while he was closing it. He thought about nothing except the closing. That is what you think about when you are making a decision you have already made. The not-thinking-beyond is part of the decision.
