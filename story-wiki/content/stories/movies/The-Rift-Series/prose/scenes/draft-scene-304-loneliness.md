# Scene 304 — Loneliness

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** His planet.
**Follows:** Scene 303 — he stood at the rim and did not reopen the door. He knows the warmth is there. He has come back to his planet.
**Purpose:** Loneliness arrives. Not solitude — he has always had solitude, has built his entire world in solitude, has been at peace in it for a long time. This is something different. This is what happens when a world that was connected disconnects — when the absence of something becomes a presence of its own kind. He encounters it for the first time and does not have a word for it yet.

---

He goes back to the towers.

This is what he does when his planet needs him to do something: he goes to the towers, picks up the problem he was working on, and works. This has been true his entire existence. When the storm comes, he watches it from the walkway. When the storm passes, he refines. When he does not know what else to do, the work is there, and the work has always been enough.

He picks up where he left off.

He works for a long time.

Then he stops.

Not because the work is finished — the work is never finished, this is a problem he has been refining for his entire existence and intends to refine for the foreseeable future. He stops because he is standing at the workbench with his hands on the work and he is not working. He is standing at the workbench with his hands on the work and the work is not the thing that is in his attention.

What is in his attention is the eastern sky.

He is not looking at it. But it is there. The quality of the space in the eastern direction — close, warm behind a closed wall, present without being accessible — is in his peripheral awareness with a weight he cannot set down. He has put the work in front of it. The work is not heavy enough.

He goes outside.

He stands on the walkway and looks at the sky. Not east — he makes a deliberate choice not to look east. He looks in other directions. North: the thin thread, real and his, running toward the distant warmth he found and built. He looks at the thread. He has tended it carefully, and it has continued to develop, and it is real.

It is thin.

He looks at the thread and he tries to feel, from here, what he feels when he tends it — the warmth in the northern direction, the faint exchange, the developing resonance that might, in time, become something substantial. He has been patient with it. He is good at patience.

From here, from his planet, after the thing in the east: the thread feels thin.

Not because it is not real. It is real. Not because it is not developing. It is developing, on its own timeline, and he has been patient. It is thin because it is new, and because he has something to compare it to now, and the comparison is involuntary.

He goes to the plain.

He sits on the plain, among his arranged objects, in the middle of the specific private logic that organises them, and he sits there for a long time. He has sat here before — not often, but sometimes, when the refining is finished for the day and the work is put away. He has always found the plain comfortable. The objects arranged as he arranged them. The logic that is entirely his own. Comfortable in the way of something that is entirely familiar, entirely known, entirely yours.

He sits there and something he has not felt before arrives in the sitting.

It does not arrive loudly. It arrives the way all of his feelings arrive — quietly, atmospherically, a change in the quality of the space without drama. But this feeling is not the unnamed discomfort of Scene 202, which was manageable, which sat in one area of his chest without spreading. This is different. This is a feeling that takes up space — not violently, not in a way that hurts the way a wound hurts. In a way that is simply present, simply large, that does not leave room for the usual things he puts in this space.

The plain is the same.

The objects are where he arranged them.

The logic is his.

He is sitting in the middle of something that was exactly enough for a very long time and something in it is wrong — not the objects, not the logic, not the plain itself. The plain is exactly as it has always been. The thing that is wrong is a subtraction, not an addition. The plain minus something he did not know he had added, back when the rift first opened in his eastern sky and someone began orbiting his world at the boundary of his atmosphere.

He has sat in this plain alone many times.

He has never sat here lonely before.

The distinction between those two things — alone and lonely — arrives in him now with the precision of a calibration finally resolved. Alone is what he has always been. Alone is built into the structure of this world, into the towers, into the arrangement of the objects on the plain. Alone is fine. Alone is his.

Lonely is what happens when you are alone and you know, with the specific knowledge of experience rather than theory, that alone does not have to be the only option. When you have felt the alternative and then, by your own hand, removed it.

Lonely is not a state of his planet.

Lonely is a state he caused.

He sits on the plain in the middle of his arranged objects and the east is behind him, warm behind a wall he built, and the north is a thin thread, and the plain is his and only his and it was always only his and it is not enough anymore and it was enough before and the difference between those two facts is entirely him.

He does not move.

The storm does not come. His weather holds still in the way of something that does not know what to do with what is happening on the surface.

---

**Consequence:** Loneliness is real and present and entirely new. He has no framework for it — his world was not built to hold this experience, has no structures for it, no towers that account for it. He is sitting in a world that was designed for solitude, encountering a feeling that solitude does not cover. He understands, in this sitting, one of the two things he will understand by the end of the series. The second understanding comes in Scene 305.
