# Scene 006 — Her World

**Format:** Silent. Visual only.
**Location:** Her planet.
**Follows:** Scene 005 — she gestured for him to follow. He followed. They crossed together.
**Purpose:** She shows him her world. He sees it properly — up close, with permission. He sees the rifts: threads going outward in multiple directions, some faint, some bright, some he cannot locate an end to. She shows him what each one is, wordlessly. By the end of the scene, he understands what all of it is — the feeling, the skill, the rift, what it means to be connected — not because she told him, but because she showed him her world and let him stand in it until he understood.

---

Her world receives them.

He notices this before anything else — the quality of arriving here with her present versus arriving here alone. Before, the space around her planet had felt occupied in a way that wasn't his, a warmth that was entirely foreign. Now the same warmth is there but it has a different character: it acknowledges him. Not because the world knows him — it doesn't, not yet — but because she knows him, and the world is her, and she is extending something of herself to include his presence.

He is allowed here now. That is what this feels like.

She leads him to the surface.

---

Her world is not orderly in the way his is. It is not disorderly either. It is *populated* — with things she brought in, things she built, things that arrived and found a place and were allowed to stay. Where his planet has the logic of a person who arranges things according to an internal system only they can fully read, hers has the logic of a person who says yes to many things and finds that they coexist, more or less, in the space she gives them. There is more colour here. More open ground that is open by choice, not because nothing has been done with it. More sky, from the surface — a bigger horizon.

And the rifts.

From the surface, the threads going outward from her world are visible as luminous lines in her atmosphere — thin, some of them, like a light seen through fabric, and thicker in others, wide enough to suggest a passage you could walk through without turning sideways. He counts them. He stops counting when the number becomes complicated. There are many.

She watches him looking at them.

She does not rush the looking. She stands beside him and lets him see what he is seeing, at the pace that he sees it, which is slow and methodical and involves returning to the same things from different angles — his characteristic approach to anything that matters.

Then she moves.

She goes to the nearest rift — one of the faint ones, a thin thread running to something distant — and she stops near its edge. She holds out one hand. Not toward the rift. Toward him.

*Look.*

He comes and stands beside her and looks at what she is showing him.

The rift is thin because its resonance is thin — a distant connection, a faint echo, something that touched the edges of her world once and left a trace. He understands this by looking at it. Not because she explains it. Because the rift looks like what it is: something real but slight, maintained by the smallest amount of continued presence.

She moves to another — thicker, brighter, pulling slightly at the atmosphere around it the way a heavy thing pulls at the space it occupies. She stands beside this one and he stands beside her and the warmth from this rift is substantial. He feels it from a metre away. Whatever is on the other side of this one is something that resonated strongly with her world, that met her reaching with its own reaching, that built something persistent.

He looks at her.

She looks at the rift.

Then she looks at him.

And in the specific way of a person who is trying to communicate something important without the use of words, she turns so that she is facing him fully, and she does something he watches very carefully: she shows him what reaching looks like. Not a dramatic gesture. Something almost invisible — a slight extension of her world's atmosphere toward one of the rift-threads, a leaning in a direction, a quality of attention directed outward. The thread responds. Brightens, fractionally.

She releases it. The brightness fades back to what it was.

She shows him again — the same gesture, same leaning, same directed attention. Same result.

She steps back. She looks at him.

His turn.

He faces the nearest rift — the thin one, the distant connection — and he tries to do what she showed him. He extends something. Not his hand, not physically. The internal equivalent: a leaning of his attention, a direction of something he has always had but never named. He has felt this before, without knowing it. In Scene 001, unknowingly. In Scene 002, learning the shape of it.

Now, consciously.

The thin rift responds. Only slightly — it is thin, the connection on the other end is faint, and he is new at this. But it responds. The thread brightens at his end by the smallest possible degree.

He holds it for a moment, then releases.

He looks at her. Something in him has shifted in the past minute — not dramatically, not permanently, but in the specific way of a first correct use of something that has been present and unrecognised for a long time. Like using a muscle you didn't know you had. It is slightly sore and entirely new.

She sees this.

She does not react with performance — no celebration, no teaching-moment emphasis. She simply nods. The specific nod of someone who has seen this before and considers it an ordinary thing, which is the most reassuring possible response.

He looks out at her world from where they are standing. The rifts in all their directions. The colours. The populated surface. The horizon that is wider than his.

He looks back at the rift that came and opened in his own world. The one she created, or the one that formed when his reaching and her noticing found each other and made something between them. He cannot see it from here — it is in his world, not hers — but he knows it is there.

He knows what it is now.

He knows what the feeling was — the warmth in one direction, the pull, the planet leaning. He knows what the rift means. He knows what reaching is, and what it produces, and what happens when two worlds reach toward each other and meet.

He does not know, yet, what comes next.

But he is standing in someone else's world, in the open, with permission, and understanding for the first time what it means that another world exists at all.

That is enough for now.

---

**Consequence:** Episode 1 ends here. He is in her world. He understands the architecture of what has happened to him over the past weeks — the rift, the feeling, the crossing, the skill. He has seen that she is connected to many worlds, in many different degrees, and he has felt, for the first time, what his own reaching does when it is used deliberately. He will return to his planet. The rift between their worlds will be there. It is the most significant thing in his space. Episode 2 begins when he goes back to her world knowing what he is doing — and finds that knowing what you are doing is only the beginning of the harder questions.
