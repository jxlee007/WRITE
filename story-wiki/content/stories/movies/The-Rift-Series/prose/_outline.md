# The Rift Series — Episode 1: Official Rift
## Scene Outline

| # | Scene | Status | What Happens |
|---|---|---|---|
| 001 | The First Rift | 🔄 Draft | He leaves his world by accident. Finds a rift already formed in the space nearby. Stands at its edge. Does not go through. Returns. |
| 002 | The Feeling | 🔄 Draft | Days later. The skill activates — he notices a warmth, a pull, a direction in the space around his planet. He rises. Faces the feeling. Does not yet reach. His planet has begun to lean. |
| 003 | The Reach | ⬜ Todo | He extends something of his world toward the source of the feeling. Effort, directed. We see what reaching looks like visually — his atmosphere stretching, his planet's gravity angling outward. Something responds. Or doesn't. |
| 004 | The Response | ⬜ Todo | The other world answers — or does not. If yes: a rift begins to form between two worlds that have chosen each other. Episode ends on the rift opening, not on entry. If no: the reach fades, the space goes dark in that direction. Episode ends on him returning to his planet alone. The rift from Scene 001 is still there, untouched. |

## Arc Notes

Episode 1 is about **awareness and approach**.

He does not begin knowing he can do any of this. He does not know the skill exists, does not know what a rift means, does not know that the feeling is navigable. He discovers each thing in sequence, the way you discover things in actual life — not through explanation, but through experience.

**The two-rift structure:** He found the first rift passively (Scene 001). The second proximity (Scene 002) may or may not produce a rift — that depends on the reach and the response (Scenes 003–004). The first rift remains present and unresolved. It is not the same story as the second proximity. Whether these threads converge, or whether the first rift belongs to Episode 2 or 3, is TBD.

**Silent film logic:** Every beat is a visual event. Emotion is carried by the movement of the planet, the quality of the space, the texture of the atmosphere — not by expression or dialogue. The audience understands what he feels because they can see what his world does.

**No resolution forced.** Scene 004 can end with the rift forming OR with rejection. Both are valid Episode 1 endings. The story's tone — whether Episode 2 is about a successful connection deepening, or about what happens after a failed reach — depends on that choice.
