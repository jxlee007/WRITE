# Sun Dimer / Daimler

**Appears In:** [Lvl 3 Power](../index.md)
**Role:** King of Spades / Antagonist → Tragic Architect
**Card Rank:** ♠ King (King of Spades)
**Classification:** Separate Actor (not a full Betrayer), Confirmed Waker (late arc)
**Conceptual Seed:** 5D Chess with Multiverse Time Travel (`raw-res/KT-260626`)
**Narrative Function:** The story's most complex figure. Begins as a survival-driven competitor who eliminates threats. Ends as the person who invented the mechanism that makes everyone's freedom possible — too late to benefit from it himself.

> **Season 1 audience presentation:** A cold, methodical elimination machine running through the competition using his 3D chess domain. He appears to be defeated/eliminated at Season 1's end. The audience takes this at face value. The truth — that he Awakened, hit the system's ceiling, and was excommunicated as a Waker — is the late-series reveal.

---

## Identity

Daimler is not a villain in the conventional sense. He is a **survivor** operating inside a system he does not yet fully understand. His strategic intelligence is exceptional. His judgment is calibrated entirely around competitive self-preservation. He kills Kaien Mugen not from cruelty but from firsthand, data-driven terror — he has already fought Kaien and barely survived.

He is the King of Spades. He leads no faction — he cooperates with the Betrayers (Diamond King's group) where interests align but answers to no one. This independence is not nobility. It is the habit of a person who has learned that alliances inside the competition are liabilities.

As his Lvl 3 matures and he accumulates time-travel experience, the competitive calculus he has lived by stops making sense. The arc of his entire character is the slow, devastating process of watching your survival strategy become irrelevant — and then deciding what to do with the understanding that replaces it.

## Power Levels

### Lvl 1 — Influence & Manipulation
Daimler can influence and manipulate people. Not mind control — a more nuanced, social and emotional leverage. He reads what people want, what they fear, what they will trade, and applies pressure at the exact point that moves them. This is how he operates his house, his alliances with the Betrayers, and his competitive positioning. He is the most politically effective person in the tournament.

At Lvl 1, this is subtle enough to be mistaken for natural charisma and leadership. Opponents and allies rarely identify it as a power at all.

### Lvl 3 — Time Infliction *(The Wandering Clock)* — Season 1 Phase: 3D Chess Domain

**Season 1 form — 3D Chess (what the audience sees):**

His Lvl 3 at Season 1 maturity manifests as a spatial Outer Realm expansion. It looks like this:

> An 8-floor building. Each floor is a perfect 8×8 grid. Every floor is 64 rooms. Every room is a position on the board.
> Total: **512 rooms** (8 × 8 × 8).

Opponents who enter the domain are inside a 3-dimensional chessboard. Daimler controls all 512 positional relationships simultaneously. He does not simply move — he *repositions the entire spatial logic* of where any given opponent is relative to every other point. Navigating toward him means navigating through a structure that is reconfiguring itself faster than the opponent can read it.

```
2D chess (8×8):      64 positions    — readable, escapable
3D chess (8×8×8):   512 positions   — Daimler Season 1 domain
4D chess (8⁴):    4,096 positions   — what his power becomes after excommunication
```

At Season 1, this is entirely spatial — no temporal axis yet. The complexity is already overwhelming for normal fighters. Only Kaien's dual-realm operation (Inner Realm spheres + Outer Realm terrain) presented a genuine threat inside it: his Inner Realm Spherical Void travels with the target and is not constrained by the 3D spatial restructuring. This is the firsthand experience that produced Daimler's fear and, eventually, the assassination.

**The evolution — why 3D becomes 4D:**

After excommunication, cast into the human world as an Unanchored entity, the spatial 3D domain dissolves (no competition grounds to anchor it). What remains is the underlying power: the ability to *navigate positions within a structured system.* In the human world, without the tournament's spatial scaffolding, those positions become **time coordinates.** He is no longer moving through rooms. He is moving through moments. The 4th axis — time — was always latent in the power. The excommunication unlocked it by removing the 3D framework that was containing it.

The Wandering Clock is not a different power than the 3D chess domain. It is the same power with one more axis added.

This is not clean, surgical time travel. It is better understood through the **5D Chess with Multiverse Time Travel** model:
- A standard move in chess is positional (2D geometry)
- A move in 5D Chess affects not just the board but the *timeline* — previous states of the board become live again, and moves in the past propagate forward
- Each of Daimler's time-travel visits **creates or alters a branching timeline** that he must then navigate in addition to all previous branches

The more he uses this power, the more timelines he has visited and affected. The system of cause-and-effect he is operating inside grows **exponentially more complex with each use.** Eventually it becomes impossible to fully comprehend — even for him.

> Standard time travel: 8×8 = 64 positions to track.
> Daimler's actual experience: 8×8×8×8 = 4,096 positions — and growing.

**What this costs him:** Cognitive coherence. The more he travels, the more he is simultaneously a person operating in the present *and* a person haunted by every version of events he has witnessed across time. He knows things that have not happened yet. He also knows things that happened in timelines that no longer exist. Separating them becomes progressively harder.

**What this gives him:** A perspective that no other fighter in the competition has. He has *seen* the tournament from the outside. He has watched what happens to winners. He has seen the Revis harvest. He has witnessed the loop.

## The Psychological Trajectory — Four Stages

### Stage 1 — The Survivor (Season 1)
Daimler's world is entirely transactional. The tournament is a hierarchy. He is near the top of it. His job is to stay there.

**The Season 1 strategy:** He burns through the competition using the 3D chess domain. The goal is simple: eliminate as many threats as possible before they can be organized against him. He is not building alliances. He is building a body count. He uses whatever available competition slots (including unnamed/empty card holders) as fuel for this campaign.

He cooperates with the Betrayers because their interests align with his survival at this stage. He is not a believer in their faction — he is a user of their shared interest.

He has **half-understanding** of the Harvesting Truth at this point. He suspects something is wrong with the tournament's endgame. He has seen things through early, incomplete Lvl 3 use that don't add up. But he doesn't have the full picture. His response to this half-knowledge is to push harder: *if winning is somehow compromised, win faster.*

This is why the excommunication is so devastating as a turning point. He was not trying to escape. He was trying to dominate. The system threw him out anyway.

**Season 1 ending — Apparent Elimination:**
At Season 1's conclusion, Daimler appears to be defeated/eliminated. The audience, the competition, and other characters read this as a clean exit — a powerful figure who overreached and was stopped.

What actually happened: **his Awakening.** His willpower driving the 3D chess domain hit the system's absolute ceiling. The math the tournament uses to process his power hit 100% and could not continue. The system excommunicated him automatically — stripped his card, cast him into the human world as a neutral ghost. He retains all his power. He is now Unanchored, Lvl 6, and locked out of the competition.

The 3D spatial domain dissolves without the competition's anchoring. What remains activates: the 4th axis. Time.

### Stage 2 — The Discovery (Mid Series)
His Lvl 3 begins revealing things he was not expecting.

He visits time points in the tournament's history and starts seeing patterns:
- Fighters who won previous cycles — where did they go?
- What happens after Lvl 4 is reached?
- Why do the Revis watch so carefully? Why is the Observer Law so absolute?

He discovers the Harvesting Truth not through alliance or intelligence but through **temporal witness.** He watches a cycle end. He watches the harvest. He sees what the Revis actually are.

The survival strategy he has built his entire competitive career on stops making sense. You can win the tournament. The Revis still take what they came for. There is no exit from inside the system.

> *"There is no point even after ending the competition."*

This is his breaking point. Not grief — clarity. The kind that costs something.

### Stage 3 — The Wandering (Late Series)
He continues traveling. Not to gain tactical advantage anymore — because the tactical frame has dissolved. He travels to understand.

He sees multiple tournament cycles. He watches different fighters, different strategies, different Wakers. He watches some people discover the truth and not know what to do with it. He watches others escape — the return to human life, the 3D existence, the ending of the loop.

**The frozen cave:** Among the time points he visits is a moment in Satyug — millions of years in the past. A cave. A body in permanent meditation, frozen in a Time Psychosis pocket where the space around it stopped moving. He understands immediately what it is: the physical body of the being who created the Shoshinsha Realm. The anchor that keeps everything running. Destroy the body, dissolve the realm.

He cannot do it. He is Unanchored, locked out of the tournament system. He cannot project force across the realm boundary into the physical world. He is standing at the solution and unable to reach it.

He is accumulating a map. The cognitive cost is accelerating. He is becoming someone who has seen too much of time to be fully present in any single moment of it.

### Stage 4 — The Inheritance (Series End)
He invents the Inheritance Technique.

Not Resonance Unchain — Kaien's technique for *how* to escape. This is the **map** that tells you *what you are escaping and why.* He lays what he calls the Origin Stone in the human world — a mechanism for transmitting the Harvesting Truth, the loop structure, and the path to freedom across competition cycles, so future fighters do not have to spend lifetimes wandering through time to arrive at what he already knows.

The Inheritance Technique is his legacy. He will not benefit from it himself. He may already be too far into the temporal complexity of his Lvl 3 to return to a clean 3D existence. But he ensures the knowledge survives him.

**The Phantom 12 conversation:** The one action Daimler takes that the Inheritance Technique alone cannot cover: he finds Phantom 12 and delivers one piece of information — that the Creator's body exists, where it is frozen in time, and that the only person capable of projecting force across the realm boundary into the physical world is Phantom 12 himself. He does not tell him what to do. He tells him who he is.

**The series ends with this:** The true winners of the tournament are not the strongest competitors. They are the ones who understand the system, escape it with their humanity intact (Resonance Unchain), and return to human life — 3D, free, permanently. The fighters who achieve this carry both Kaien's technique and Daimler's map. Neither alone is enough.

## The 2D → 3D → 2D Framework

Daimler's arc is the story's clearest articulation of the cycle all fighters are trapped in:

| State | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **3D — Before bonding** | Full human existence. Dimensional, free, present. Infinite directions. |
| **Card bonding** | The compression begins. Identity starts to be defined by the card's mathematical position. |
| **Tournament competition** | Progressive flattening. You *become* what your rank says you are. |
| **Lvl 4 / Harvest** | Fully 2D. Compressed into fuel. The person is extracted and consumed. |
| **Reset** | A new cycle begins. Different fighters, same loop. |
| **True escape** | Resonance Unchain (Kaien's technique) + Inheritance map (Daimler's technique) = permanent return to 3D |

Daimler has seen this loop across time. He has watched fighters he knew in one cycle appear in different forms in the next. He has seen the tournament reproduce itself century after century. His Lvl 3 is what showed him the full architecture of the trap.

> *"It would be something experiencing kind of a personality from 2D to 3D and back going to 2D."*

This is what he sees in time: every fighter is 3D before the card finds them, and 2D by the end. The only exception is a successful escape. He has seen those too — rare, almost impossible, but real. Those are the ones he builds his Inheritance Technique around.

## The Tragic Irony — What He Did to Kaien

Daimler eliminated Kaien Mugen — who independently discovered Resonance Unchain, the *how* of escape — because Kaien's combat power threatened him.

At the time of the assassination, Daimler did not know about Resonance Unchain. He did not know Kaien had found half the solution. He killed the person carrying the complementary half of what he would eventually spend years in time discovering for himself.

The specific irony:
- Kaien had the escape *method* (Resonance Unchain)
- Daimler would eventually have the escape *map* (Inheritance Technique)
- Together, they had the complete solution
- Daimler's fear ended that possibility before it could become real

By the time Daimler understands what Kaien was, Kaien is long dead. The Inheritance Technique he creates is, in part, an attempt to complete what Kaien started — without ever having known Kaien was already there.

## Relationships

- **Kaien Mugen (♣ Ace)** — Killed him. Later understands what he destroyed. The most painful knowledge his time travel eventually delivers.
- **The Betrayers (Diamond King's faction)** — Used as cover and shared-interest alliance during Stage 1. Becomes irrelevant as his arc progresses into Stage 3.
- **Sato (♣ King)** — The second anomaly. No direct relationship. His Inheritance Technique is the map Sato's circle uses to complete the escape.
- **The Revis (Group of 7)** — Witnessed the harvest through temporal travel. His Inheritance Technique is built to undermine their pipeline.
- **The Shoshinsha Creator** — Encountered in the frozen cave via time travel. Understands what the body is and what destroying it would do. Cannot act. Carries this knowledge as the heaviest burden of his wandering.
- **Phantom 12** — Delivers the one piece of intelligence Phantom 12 needs: the Creator's body exists, and Phantom 12 is the only person who can reach it.
