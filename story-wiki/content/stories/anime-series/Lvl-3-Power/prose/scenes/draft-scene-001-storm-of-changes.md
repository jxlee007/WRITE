# Scene 001 — Storm of Changes

**Location:** Grand-Emo Storm's training ground. A flat plateau of cracked earth, somewhere high and empty. No spectators. No officials. One old man, one young fighter, and the wind.
**Characters:** Sato (newcomer), Grand-Emo Storm (retired grandmaster)
**Purpose:** Sato demands the Storm of Changes trial. Emo refuses. Sato pushes anyway. The moment that defines whether Sato is genuinely insane or genuinely extraordinary — and the answer turns out to be both.

---

The old man was sitting with his back to him, eating rice from a bowl, when Sato arrived.

"I'm closing," Emo said, without turning. "Come back in a decade."

Sato stopped walking. He'd climbed for three hours to reach this plateau. His shoes were destroyed. He was not coming back in a decade.

"I want the Storm of Changes trial."

Emo set his bowl down.

He turned around. He was small — people always forgot that about him, given everything they'd heard. Small, and older than the mountain he lived on, and possessed of the kind of stillness that you only acquire by having been violent for a very long time and then stopping.

He looked Sato over. The kid was maybe twenty-three. Intermediate rank by the calluses on his knuckles — maybe mid-card clubs, no higher. Standing here asking for a grandmaster-level domain trial like it was a bus ticket.

"You know what the Storm of Changes is," Emo said.

"Yes."

"You know what it does to people who aren't ready."

"Yes."

"You know that no mortal has ever passed it."

"I know."

Emo tilted his head a fraction. "Then you understand why I'm going to say no."

"I understand why you want to." Sato held his gaze. "I'm asking you to do it anyway."

The wind moved across the plateau. A long silence.

"Give me one reason," Emo said.

"Because the clubs house needs a King. Because whoever wins your trial gets the right to challenge for it. And because every fighter with a legitimate claim is sitting on their ranking and waiting for someone else to go first." A beat. "I'm not waiting."

"The last person who said something like that to me lost the use of his left arm."

"I've heard."

"And?"

Sato said nothing. He just stood there, in his ruined shoes, with his hands loose at his sides, waiting.

Emo looked at him for a long time. Then he stood up, and the stillness fell off him the way a coat falls off someone who's been waiting all evening to take it off.

"Don't scream," Emo said. "It makes the terrain worse."

He raised one finger.

The plateau cracked open.

*What followed was forty minutes that Sato would never describe accurately to anyone, because there are no accurate words for having your emotional body used as a weapon against itself. The Storm of Changes does not test power. It tests the thing underneath power — the specific grief or rage or love that a fighter uses as fuel — and it turns that thing inside out and asks: what are you when the fuel is gone?*

*Most people find nothing. The nothing breaks them.*

*Sato found something.*

*It wasn't peace. It was hunger — pure and old and structurally sound. He wanted power the way other people wanted air, and when the Storm stripped everything else away, the hunger was still there. Intact. Unashamed. Enough to build on.*

*Emo dropped the domain when he saw it. He'd been ready to stop it — three times in forty minutes he'd had his hand at the edge of the seal, prepared to pull Sato out. He hadn't needed to.*

*He picked up his rice bowl. It was cold now.*

"Intermediate newbie passes the Storm of Changes," he said, more to himself than to Sato, who was on one knee and breathing hard. "First mortal to ever do it."

Sato looked up.

"The clubs King challenge," he said. His voice was ragged but his eyes were steady. "When can I—"

"Tomorrow," Emo said. "Let your hands stop shaking first."

---

**Consequence:** Sato passes the Storm of Changes — the unprecedented event that reshuffles every assumption the 52-card competition holds about the clubs house. Emo takes him on as a disciple not out of sentimentality, but because a fighter who runs toward the thing that should break him is either useless to everyone or useful to everyone, and Emo has lived long enough to want to find out which.
