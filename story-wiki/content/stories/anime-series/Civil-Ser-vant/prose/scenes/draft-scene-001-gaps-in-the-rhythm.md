# Scene 001 — Gaps in the Rhythm

**Location:** Upstack Transit Hub, Mag-Line Platform 7. Late in the DEVICE QUIET HOURS window — 23 minutes remaining. The neon is tired, flickering at the edges. The crowd moves in the synchronized pulse of people who do not know they are synchronized.
**Characters:** The Awakened (Protagonist), Craze
**Purpose:** The protagonist slips through a patrol window and sits near a stranger on a bench. The stranger sees him. In a city where surveillance is total, being *seen* — not scanned, seen — is the most dangerous thing that can happen.

---

The firmware had been active in its ambient synchronization state for six minutes.

The protagonist could feel it behind his left eye—a pressure that wasn't quite pain, but a subtle integration: a quiet expansion of his senses, an overlay of code coexisting seamlessly with his own neural pathways. The patrol cycle was forty-two seconds. He'd counted it three times, standing at the transit map kiosk with his fingers tracing routes he didn't need, until the numbers resolved into a geometry he could move through.

*Gap at thirty-one,* the Assistant said. Text only. No voice. The holographic rendering bled into his peripheral vision like a second thought. *Eleven seconds. Platform B side-entry. Move.*

He moved.

The gap was exactly eleven seconds. He felt the displacement of air as the CCC drone passed behind him — close enough that its sensor array would have caught him if he'd been standing still. He wasn't standing still. He was already on the bench, sitting, breathing evenly, watching a pigeon navigate the underside of the mag-rail with more freedom than any of the humans below it.

The bench was corroded at the joints. Platform 7 was Downstack infrastructure wearing an Upstack maintenance schedule — cleaned on paper, falling apart in practice. The man at the other end of the bench appeared to be asleep.

Appeared to be.

The protagonist had catalogued him on the approach: mid-forties, civilian registry clothes that were slightly too correct in their neutrality, a small scuff on his left boot that suggested he'd recently moved through somewhere unpaved. Badge 007 on his sleeve, visible only at a specific angle. The eyes behind the half-lowered lids were tracking the patrol drone as it completed its cycle.

*Former operative,* the Assistant noted. *Registered as inactive. No Decree flags. No current surveillance priority.*

The protagonist did not look at him. He watched the pigeon.

Then the man on the bench looked at him.

Not a scan — the firmware would have logged a scan, flagged the biometric probe. This was something older than firmware. Two people in a surveilled city recognizing in each other the particular stillness of someone who knows exactly how to be looked at without being found.

The protagonist felt the back of his neck tighten.

*Don't engage,* the Assistant said.

The man gave the smallest possible nod. Five degrees of head tilt, held for less than a second, then his gaze returned to the middle distance. A nod that said: *I see you. I'm not going to do anything about it. You should probably leave.*

The protagonist stood up.

The protagonist did not look back. He reached the exit corridor and paused at the junction, the way civilians paused at junctions, deciding between identical options.

The firmware remained perfectly integrated and active.

*Who was he?* the protagonist thought, not to the Assistant — just into the interior space where thoughts went when he didn't want them parsed and categorized.

The Assistant didn't answer, because it couldn't. What he'd felt sitting on that bench wasn't a data problem. It was something he didn't have a framework for yet.

*A person who knows the gaps in the rhythm,* he thought. *Like me. But older. And much more tired.*

He turned left. The corridor swallowed him.

---

**Consequence:** The protagonist reaches his exit intact. But the firmware's alert state doesn't drop — the encounter has registered something that the system cannot categorize. Craze, for his part, has identified what he needed to identify: the firmware is active, the carrier is young, and the carrier is already learning to move through the Decree's blind spots without help. This changes Craze's calculation about how much time he has before the Mesh locates the inheritance it has been looking for.
