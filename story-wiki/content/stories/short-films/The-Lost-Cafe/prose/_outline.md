## Locked Rules

**The 3-9-3 Rule:** Act 1 is Arjun A's real life (warm, unhighlighted). Act 2 is the 9-minute dream (Arjun B — same man, same routines, different chair). Act 3 is the café ending (3 minutes, no words needed).

**The Café Position Rule:** The café in the dream must land exactly at the midpoint of Act 2 — 6 minutes in. This gives the audience time to forget it before Act 3 calls it back.

**The Hidden Regular Rule:** In Scene 003, the barista must nod or show recognition when Arjun A walks in. It must be visible but not underlined. The audience may miss it the first time. On reflection it reframes the ending entirely.

**The No-Narrator Rule:** Nothing is stated. The filled chair is the moral. Trust it.

**The Meera Rule:** She does not show up because she knew he was there. She shows up because the café is hers too. This must feel completely natural and slightly random — the serendipity of two people who share a life without tracking each other.

**The Physique Contrast Rule:** Arjun A — lean, morning run/walk, yoga, stretching. Fitness as balance. Arjun B — built, gym, weights, bodybuilder circle. Fitness as control. Same discipline, opposite engine. The body tells you which version of the man you are watching before anything else does.

**The Tea Vendor / Barista Rule:** The tea vendor working outside the café in Act 3 (reality) is the same actor as the barista inside the café in Act 2 (the dream). Same face, slightly different position. Must be visible enough to register on rewatch. This detail confirms that the dream was not invented — it was built from the same people and city, shifted slightly. Plant it quietly. Never explain it.
