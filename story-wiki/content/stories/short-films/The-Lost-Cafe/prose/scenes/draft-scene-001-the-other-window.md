# Scene 001 — First Dream

**Location:** Opens in Pune. A small apartment, morning light. Then: Bengaluru, the flat, 2 AM.
**Characters:** Arjun (experienced as Arjun-2 inside the dream — the audience doesn't know this yet), She (unnamed, present only in the dream)
**Purpose:** The audience opens in warmth and doesn't know it's a dream. The snap to the empty flat is the first gut punch. The contrast is the entire film in miniature.

---

Morning light in Pune.

The apartment is small enough that you can tell it is genuinely lived in — not decorated, *lived in*, the way places look when two people have been in them long enough to stop noticing what they've left where. A jacket on the back of a chair. A half-read book face-down on the arm of the sofa. The particular comfortable disorder of a life that has enough to occupy it.

He is at the kitchen counter, waiting for the chai to finish.

She is still asleep. He can hear her — not a sound exactly, just the absence of sound that belongs to her, the quality of the flat being occupied in a specific direction. He has lived with this long enough to find it orienting rather than intrusive. He knows which silences are his and which are hers.

The chai starts to steam.

He pours two cups. He adds the ginger — she always says too much, he always adds the same amount, she always takes the cup anyway. This is a negotiation that has been ongoing for four years and neither of them intends to resolve it.

He sets her cup on the counter for when she wakes.

He takes his to the window. The city is already moving — autorickshaws, the sound of someone's morning music from three floors down, the light doing what Pune light does at this hour, which is softer than you'd expect and goes quickly. He leans against the window frame with the cup and looks at it.

He is not thinking about anything in particular.

This is the thing about contentment — it doesn't announce itself. It just is: a cup of chai, a window, the sound of the city, the particular silence of someone asleep in the other room who is glad you are there. He doesn't think: *I am happy.* He just stands at the window and the morning exists around him and that is enough.

---

He wakes up.

The ceiling is wrong.

Not the Pune ceiling — off-white with a small water stain near the light fitting that he knows the exact shape of. This ceiling is smooth and the light is off and the flat is completely quiet in the specific way of a place that contains only one person.

He lies there and does not move.

Outside: the ambient sound of Bengaluru at 2 AM. Not silence — never silence, the city doesn't stop — but a different frequency than daytime. Slower. Further away. He has been awake at this hour many times. He knows the sound.

He does not know, for a moment, which life he just left.

Then he does.

He sits up. The flat is exactly as he left it before sleep — everything in its place, clean surfaces, the cup by the sink. The kind of order that comes from having nothing to disorder. He gets up and goes to the kitchen and runs the tap and stands there with his hands under the water.

His hands need something to do.

He fills the cup and empties it. Fills it again. The motion is not about the cup.

He stands at the sink for a long time.

The ginger. That's the detail that stays — not her face, not the apartment, not the specifics of anything that happened. Just the smell of ginger chai that isn't here, that has never been here, that belongs to a morning in a city he has not visited in five years.

He turns the tap off.

He puts the cup down.

He cannot tell anyone this. Not because they wouldn't believe him — they wouldn't, but that's not the reason. The reason is that describing it would require describing the apartment, and the apartment has her in it, and he hasn't said her name out loud since the last time he said it and it meant something.

He goes back to bed.

He lies in the dark and looks at the ceiling — his ceiling, the right ceiling, the one he has looked at for five years — and he waits for the morning.

He is afraid to go back to sleep.

He knows he will.

---

**Consequence:** This was the first night. He has no framework for it — no word for what happened, no way to categorise it, no person to tell. He knows only that it was not a dream in the ordinary sense. It was too specific. Too continuous. Too weighted with the particular sensory texture of a life that is being lived rather than imagined. The ginger is the proof of this — he has not thought about ginger chai in five years, has not bought ginger in five years, has no reason to have constructed that detail. It arrived from somewhere. He does not know where. He will sleep tomorrow. The next night will be longer. The next morning will be harder to return from.
