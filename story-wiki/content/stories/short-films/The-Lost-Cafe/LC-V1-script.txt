THE LOST CAFE'S
Written by JSM
PG - 1
FADE IN:
EXT. CITY STREET - CHAI KI TAPRI - EARLY MORNING – REALITY A
Steam curls in the chill air. RAJU CHACHA (60s, kind-eyed,
salt-and-pepper beard) pours tea for a small crowd.
ARJUN (early 30s, handsome in a pensive way, eyes carrying old wounds)
stands silently at the corner, watching commuters rush past.
Raju Chacha hands him a glass of chai before he asks.
RAJU CHACHA
Ek cutting, kam meetha -- jaante hi hoon!
ARJUN
(soft smile)
Yehi toh Khas baat hai yaah ki, Chacha.
Arjun leans against the stall, sipping his tea. Across the street: THE
LOST CAFE glows in the morning light, its windows misted, the tables
inside empty except for UNCLE RAVI (60s, bustling, a silent observer).
Arjun’s gaze lingers on the cafe, as always.
Raju Chacha wipes his hands, glancing at Arjun’s familiar routine.
RAJU CHACHA
(beta, paanch saal se roz yahin, hai na?)
Five years you come, every morning. But why all these years just
watching that cafe?
ARJUN
(covering pain with a laugh)

Bas aise hi, lagta hai fancy hai. Coffee ka budget nahi hai.
RAJU CHACHA
(teasing)
Arey, ek din chal hi jao andar. Kabhi treat toh karo apne aap ko.
ARJUN
(hollow)
Kuch jagah bas dekhne ke liye hoti hain, Chacha.
Arjun finishes his chai, nods goodbye.
ARJUN (CONT’D)
Kal milte hain.
RAJU CHACHA
(smiling, still puzzled)
Haan, roz ki tarah.
Arjun walks away, his eyes flicker once more at the cafe he can’t
enter.

PG - 2

Arjun walks briskly, briefcase in hand, blending into the flow of
suited professionals. His face is composed, almost serene, but his
eyes are distant.
INT. OFFICE FLOOR - DAY
Open-plan office, bustling yet sterile. Arjun sits at his desk,
multiple screens displaying advertising campaigns. He types
methodically, focused but mechanically.
SAMEER (30s, jovial, empathetic) approaches holding a briefcase and
pulls up a chair beside Arjun.
SAMEER
(smiling)

na.
Isiliye busy rehna padta hai kaam me.
SAMEER
(taking his chair sitting beside Arjun)
tere jaisa banda dhoondhne se bhi nahi milega isse company ko.
Ek terko hi maine dekha itne saalon me, jisne apne kaam ko itna
sambhalke kiya ho.
Arjun nods modestly, but his eyes betray a hidden ache.

SAMEER (CONT’D)
Chal ab presentation bana lete hai nai to boss aake khursi or job dono
khichlega
Sameer pats Arjun's shoulder and starts working on his desk.
Arjun exhales slowly, returning to his screens.
CUT TO:

PG - 3
INT. ARJUN'S APARTMENT - REALITY A - EVENING
A modest one-bedroom apartment. Clean but soulless. Everything in its
place, nothing out of order.
Arjun enters, loosening his tie. He sets his briefcase down with
practiced precision — same spot, every day.
The apartment is silent. No music, no voices, no life.
KITCHEN CORNER
Arjun opens the refrigerator. Minimal contents: bottled water, takeout
containers, a single apple.
He pulls out a container of leftover food, microwaves it without
looking.
LIVING AREA
He sits on the couch, eating mechanically while scrolling through his
phone. Work emails. More work emails.
The TV is off. The silence is oppressive.
His eyes drift to the window — the city lights outside, couples
walking together on the street below.
He looks away quickly.
BEDROOM - LATER
Arjun brushes his teeth, staring at himself in the bathroom mirror.
For a moment, something flickers in his eyes — a question, a memory, a
longing.

He shakes it off, rinses his mouth.
He changes into sleep clothes, the routine automatic.
The bed is made perfectly — only one side ever slept in. The other
side pristine, untouched.
Arjun lies down, staring at the ceiling.
His phone buzzes. A work notification. He ignores it.
He closes his eyes.
The silence wraps around him like a shroud.
FADE TO BLACK.
ARJUN (V.O.)
(barely a whisper)
Five years...
CUT TO:

PG - 4
DREAM SEQUENCE - INT. THE LOST CAFE - NIGHT
Soft, golden light. Everything slightly ethereal, dreamlike. Colors
are more saturated than reality.
ARJUN sits at a table by the window. But he's different here —
relaxed, genuinely smiling, alive.
Across from him sits a WOMAN (late 20s, we see her only in soft focus,
her face not quite clear). Her laughter fills the space like music.
WOMAN
(warmth in her voice)
You always get that look when you're planning something.
ARJUN
(grinning, in love)
What look?
WOMAN
That mischievous one. Like you're about to surprise me.
ARJUN
Maybe I am.
He reaches across the table, takes her hand. The gesture is so
natural, so familiar — five years of intimacy in one touch.
UNCLE RAVI (in the dream, younger, smiling) brings them coffee. Two
cups. Their cups.
UNCLE RAVI
Your usual table is always waiting.
The woman squeezes Arjun's hand.

WOMAN
Should we tell them our news?
ARJUN
(hesitant but happy)
Let's wait. I want to enjoy this moment.
She leans forward, her face coming slightly into focus — beautiful,
radiant, HOME.
WOMAN
I love you.
ARJUN
(completely certain)
Always.
The cafe glows with impossible warmth. This is happiness. This is
belonging. This is everything.
SUDDENLY — the Alarm SOUNDS.
END DREAM SEQUENCE
INT. ARJUN'S BEDROOM - REALITY A - EARLY MORNING
Arjun's eyes SNAP OPEN. He's breathing heavily, disoriented.
He sits up in bed, hand to his chest. The apartment is moon-lit and
silent.
ARJUN
(whispered, confused)
What... what was that?
He looks around his empty bedroom. The other side of the bed —
untouched.

He runs his hand over his face, trying to shake off the dream.
But the feeling lingers. The warmth. The happiness. Her.
ARJUN (CONT'D)
(to himself, disturbed)
Why now? After all this time...
He picks up his phone from the nightstand. 3:17 AM.
He stares at the ceiling, unable to go back to sleep.
The dream felt too real. Too vivid. Too... right.
FADE TO:

(hollow)

Then - he lowers phone slowly. Still only BACK visible.
ONE MINUTE WAIT - ANXIOUS MOVEMENT

TC doesn't move for five seconds. Then —
His shoulders SHAKE. Once. Twice.
He stands abruptly. Paces to the window. Still BACK TO CAMERA.
Stares at the empty ledge where pigeons were.
His hand forms a fist. Releases. Forms again.
He turns, paces back to desk. Sits. Stands again.
Restless. Agitated. Jealousy eating him alive.
He grabs his phone. Puts it down. Grabs it again.
Unlocks it. Screen glow on his unseen face.
Opens contacts. Scrolls.
We see (over shoulder): "बाबा"
His thumb hovers. Still BACK TO US.
Internal war. Desperation vs. pride. Desperation vs. consequence.
Ten seconds of hovering.
Then —
He presses CALL.
