## Wars / Conflicts / Plot Devices

Here are war / conflict types you can use, drawn from real world and adapted to your fictional universe:

* **Proxy Wars**: State powers using militias / non-state actors in another country to advance interests without open war. (India ↔ Middle East ↔ America ↔ Russia)
* **Espionage / Counter-Intelligence Conflicts**: Secret programs, identity erasure, hidden records, betrayals.
* **Assassinations / Sacrifices**: Male friend’s sacrifice; betrayals by officers or corrupt politicians.
* **Government Corruption Deals**: Corrupt officers, politicians selling deals, illegal arms deals, fake bail, shell companies.
* **False Death / Illusion**: Protagonist declared dead; international fake documents; staged building explosion.
* **Secret Communications**: Secure numbers, satellite comms, hidden leaks, redacted documents.

## Key Themes & Motifs to Ground the Fiction

* **Identity & Erasure**: What it means to exist, or not exist. Being “officially dead.”
* **Vengeance vs. Peace**: Protagonist driven by revenge, but revenge doesn't fill void.
* **Shadow Reality vs Public Narrative**: What people believe vs what’s true. (E.g. female friend believing he’s dead; public assuming deaths after explosion.)
* **Power Structures Invisible to Public Eye**: Mafia’s influence, secret RAW programs, black sites.
* **Symbols / Recurrent Numbers**: e.g. “80 lacs” bail; redacted seals; CCTV blackouts; “80” as motif.
* **Sacrifice & Loss**: Male friend’s death; female friend’s loss; protagonist’s emotional cost.
