For centuries, an Indian royal household has guarded a secret technique that grants immortality. It is more than a miracle. It is a tradition used to protect power, preserve wealth, and ensure control of the family lineage. With every new generation, the ritual is repeated: the “new head” inherits the family’s fortune and authority, while the older generation continues to exist, untouched by time.

By the fourth generation, the system begins to crack. One child questions the cost of living forever and rejects the royal technique, choosing instead to live a natural human life, like ordinary people. The elders see this refusal as anomaly and pressure them to undergo the ritual, insisting they are the best choice to lead the next generation. But they stand firm, telling the family to choose another heir.

When the fifth generation is born, tensions sharpen into something darker. A conspiracy forms within the household to ensure the immortality formula remains in the family’s control. Even the fourth-generation parent chooses normal living, trying to train the next generation while also defending a more human, natural life.

As loyalty, ambition, and fear collide, the family faces its most dangerous conflict yet: whether the fifth generation should inherit immortality or finally break the cycle. This struggle becomes the heart of Amarta, where the opportunities of having eternal life disrupts the natural living way of life and importance of bonds.
