# Gumsum puppet

Date: December 29, 2025
Tags: Short film

Inspired by the mood of the song *Gumsum Gumsum*, 

the film follows a gifted puppet maker who takes pride in being "the best of the best." His exceptional artistry has made him wealthy and renowned across lands for his near-to-real craftsmanship. He owns a grand museum dedicated to his work, where he showcases his art and houses his extensive collection. The museum also features a theatre stage inside, where he presents his creations to captivated audiences.

His devotion turns into obsession when he begins sculpting **Saheli**, a breathtaking female puppet. As Saheli takes shape, he starts projecting his loneliness onto her, mistaking his craftsmanship for connection. He falls in love with his own creation, blurring the line between art and reality.

The illusion collapses during a test performance set in a theatre, evoking the energy of classic stage shows and local puppet traditions. In an ambitious dance move, he swings Saheli with force, trying to make her move like a living partner. The strain overwhelms the puppet’s joints. Saheli shatters in front of the audience.

In that moment, the puppet maker is left facing the truth: no matter how perfect the craft, a puppet cannot replace a real human presence. The performance ends, and he stands alone in the aftermath—surrounded by his masterpieces, yet confronted by the emptiness he tried to hide.

The setting draws inspiration from an Indian puppet performance space, with visual and cultural cues reminiscent of *Dil Lagaliya Maine Tumse Pyaar Karke*–style stage presentations.
